Early English practice works best when it feels familiar, repeatable, and easy for the parent to extend beyond the screen.

English 1 is positioned for vocabulary and early language confidence. Parents can use it as a short warm-up, then ask the child to say the word, find a matching object at home, or trace a related shape on paper. That small bridge from screen to room is where practice becomes more memorable.
