The 7 Layers of the OSI Model

Each OSI layer serves a unique task for network communication. We break down exactly how your data travels from a physical wire directly to your web browser.

Layer 7 - Application Layer: Top Level APIs, network services, and process-to-process data (e.g. HTTP, DNS).
Layer 6 - Presentation Layer: Data conversion, abstraction, formatting, encryption and decryption.
Layer 5 - Session Layer: Opening, closing, and managing synchronized sessions between apps.
Layer 4 - Transport Layer: Reliability, flow control, port multiplexing, TCP and UDP.
Layer 3 - Network Layer: IP Addressing, packet forwarding, routing tables, and global paths.
Layer 2 - Data Link Layer: MAC addressing, framing, collision checking, and local LAN delivery.
Layer 1 - Physical Layer: Raw bit streams (0s and 1s), physical cables, Wi-Fi hardware, and electrical signals.

Learn to Remember: Popular OSI Acronyms

If you are studying for your CCNA or CompTIA, here are the famous acronyms to remember the layers starting from Layer 7 down to Layer 1:

  • All People Seem To Need Data Protection (Layer 1 -> 7)
  • Please Do Not Throw Sausage Pizza Away (Layer 7 -> 1)
  • Programmers Dare Not Throw Salty Pretzels Away (Layer 7 -> 1)
